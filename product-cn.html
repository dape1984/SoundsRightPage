1. Hanzi (汉字) Learning

Master Chinese characters with comprehensive details and structured practice. 3,000 characters organized by official HSK levels 1–7, covering the complete character set from China's merged proficiency standard.

HSK 1–7 Progressive Levels

Characters are grouped by HSK level, providing a clear learning path from beginner to advanced:

  • HSK 1 — 300 foundational characters for basic communication Free
  • HSK 2 — 300 characters for simple daily interactions Free
  • HSK 3 — 300 characters for intermediate conversation Free
  • HSK 4 — 300 characters for complex topics and discussions Pro
  • HSK 5 — 300 characters for reading newspapers and academic texts Pro
  • HSK 6 — 300 characters for advanced proficiency Pro
  • HSK 7 — 1,200 characters for near-native mastery Pro

Each level is self-contained, so you can start at any level that matches your ability.

Rich Character Details

Every character entry includes:

  • Stroke count — precise count from the Unihan database
  • Radical — the root component for dictionary lookup
  • Pinyin — with tone marks for accurate pronunciation
  • Definitions — in both Chinese and English
  • Example words — compound words containing the character
  • Example sentences — with pinyin and English translation

Practice & Test Modes

Practice with customizable session sizes (1–300 characters). Test your recognition and recall. Handwriting bands are grouped into elementary, intermediate, and advanced tiers for character writing practice.

2. Ci (词语) Vocabulary

Build your Chinese vocabulary with 9,443 words and phrases organized by HSK level and character groups. Words are connected to the characters you're learning, reinforcing both simultaneously.

Three Vocabulary Types

  • Common Words (常用词) — everyday vocabulary essential for daily communication
  • Idioms (成语) — four-character Chinese idioms with rich cultural meaning
  • Proverbs (谚语) — traditional Chinese sayings passed down through generations

Each word includes detailed definitions in both Chinese and English, part of speech, and related word suggestions.

Character-Grouped Organization

Words are organized by the characters they contain, so as you learn a new character, you simultaneously discover all the words built from it. This approach mirrors how native speakers build vocabulary — through character combinations rather than isolated word lists.

Practice & Test

Customizable practice sessions with adjustable word counts. Test mode assesses your grasp of meaning, pronunciation, and usage. Performance is tracked across all HSK levels.

5. Hanzi Test (汉字测试)

Reinforce character recognition and recall with three timed test modes. Each question has a 10-second timer and auto-advances after answering.

4 Test Modes

1 Listen & Choose Character (听句选字) — Hear a sentence, then choose the correct character from options
2 Fill in the Blank (填空) — Read a sentence with a missing character and select the right one
3 Listen & Choose Meaning (听音选义) — Hear a character and pick its correct definition
4 Listen & Identify (听句辨字) — Hear a sentence and identify the target character within it

Auto-Advance & Wordbook Integration

After selecting an answer, feedback is shown for 1.2 seconds before automatically moving to the next question. Characters you answer incorrectly are automatically added to your Wordbook for later review. A score of 60% or higher counts as passed.

Customizable Sessions

Choose which HSK levels to test, enable or disable individual test modes, and set the number of questions per session. Track your accuracy and per-mode statistics after each test.

7. Dialogue Test (对话测试)

Test your spoken Chinese under time pressure. Record your part in real dialogues and receive multi-dimensional pronunciation scoring for each line.

A/B Speaker Recording

Play the role of Speaker A, Speaker B, or both. TTS plays the other speaker's lines, and you record yours. Each recording is automatically scored with a timeout based on line length.

Multi-Dimensional Scoring

Every line is evaluated on four dimensions:

  • Accuracy — correctness of individual character pronunciation and tones
  • Fluency — natural rhythm, pacing, and connected speech
  • Pronunciation — tonal precision for Mandarin's four tones plus neutral tone
  • Completeness — whether all characters in the target line were spoken

Character-Level Color Feedback

After each attempt, recognized characters are color-coded — green for correct, red for wrong — so you can see exactly which characters need more practice.

Detailed Results

After completing a dialogue, swipe through a line-by-line breakdown showing scores for each exchange. Compare your recording against the target to identify specific weaknesses.
